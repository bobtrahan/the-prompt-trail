## 1. Critical Bug: TIMER_BY_DAY Is Dead Code

**Severity: CRITICAL — the entire difficulty curve doesn't exist in-game.**

`TUNING.TIMER_BY_DAY` defines per-day timers (75s → 34s) but the `ExecutionScene` timer setup at line ~782 uses `TUNING.BASE_TIMER_SECONDS` (always 45) or `TUNING.CORP_TIMER_SECONDS` (always 22). The per-day values are only referenced in one place: the mid-execution event preview calculation (line ~1519), which is a display-only path.

**Current (ExecutionScene.ts, ~line 782):**
```ts
const baseTimer = state.playerClass === 'corporateDev'
  ? TUNING.CORP_TIMER_SECONDS
  : TUNING.BASE_TIMER_SECONDS;
```

**Proposed:**
```ts
const dayBase = (TUNING.TIMER_BY_DAY as Record<number, number>)[state.day]
  ?? TUNING.BASE_TIMER_SECONDS;
const baseTimer = state.playerClass === 'corporateDev'
  ? Math.round(dayBase * TUNING.CORP_TIMER_RATIO)
  : dayBase;
```

**Also update PlanningScene.ts (~line 425)** to match — the timer preview must use `TIMER_BY_DAY[state.day]`, not `BASE_TIMER_SECONDS`.

**Reasoning:** Without this fix, every day plays on a flat 45s timer. Days 1–6 are trivially easy (under 5 cps needed), and days 9–13 are physically impossible even for a 100 WPM typist. There is no difficulty curve at all.

## 2. Timer Retuning: The Difficulty Curve

Once `TIMER_BY_DAY` is wired, the values themselves need reworking. The current values were designed for shorter prompts; the actual prompt content creates a very different char-demand curve.

### Typing Demand Analysis (60 WPM = 5 chars/sec baseline)

| Day | Prompts | Total Chars | Design Zone | Target CPS | Current Timer | Proposed Timer |
|-----|---------|-------------|-------------|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| 1   | 4       | 39          | Tutorial    | ~1.0      | 75s           | **40s**        |
| 2   | 4       | 44          | Tutorial    | ~1.1      | 72s           | **40s**        |
| 3   | 4       | 48          | Tutorial    | ~1.3      | 70s           | **38s**        |
| 4   | 4       | 119         | Easy        | ~2.8      | 58s           | **42s**        |
| 5   | 5       | 177         | Moderate    | ~3.5      | 55s           | **50s**        |
| 6   | 5       | 203         | Moderate    | ~4.1      | 52s           | **50s**        |
| 7   | 6       | 282         | Pressure    | ~4.7      | 50s           | **60s**        |
| 8   | 6       | 249         | Pressure    | ~4.5      | 44s           | **55s**        |
| 9   | 7       | 325         | Hard        | ~5.0      | 42s           | **65s**        |
| 10  | 8       | 453         | Hard        | ~5.2      | 40s           | **88s**        |
| 11  | 8       | 598         | Brutal      | ~5.5      | 38s           | **110s**       |
| 12  | 9       | 676         | Brutal      | ~5.6      | 36s           | **120s**       |
| 13  | 10      | 731         | Impossible  | ∞         | 34s           | **60s**        |

**Design intent at each tier:**

- **Tutorial (Days 1–3):** Timer is generous enough that a 30 WPM typist finishes with time to spare. Prompts are short — any experienced gamer completes them. But the timer isn't *so* long it feels like dead air. 38–40s means a slow typer finishes in ~15s and has 25s of overtime to earn bonus rep (feels rewarding, teaches the overtime mechanic).
- **Easy (Day 4):** First jump in prompt length (avg 30 chars). A 60 WPM typist finishes with ~18s to spare. Teaches that longer prompts eat more time.
- **Moderate (Days 5–6):** 5-prompt days. A 60 WPM typist finishes with 10–15s to spare — enough for 2–3 overtime prompts. Sloppy typing costs you overtime, but doesn't prevent completion.
- **Pressure (Days 7–8):** A 60 WPM typist can complete all prompts but has almost no overtime margin. Accuracy and speed both matter. Strategy bonuses (+6s from planThenBuild) feel meaningful here.
- **Hard (Days 9–10):** A 60 WPM typist can *just barely* complete all prompts. At 50 WPM, you'll miss 1–2 prompts. The power curve means missing 2/8 prompts drops you to 60% progress — a real penalty.
- **Brutal (Days 11–12):** A 60 WPM typist needs every second. At 50 WPM, you're completing 6/8 or 7/9 prompts. Strategy and model choices are the difference between B-rank and D-rank on these days.
- **Impossible (Day 13):** Bumped to 60s (from 34s). 731 chars in 60s = 12.2 cps = 146 WPM. Still impossible for any human typist, but the extra time means players get further into the prompts before chaos hits, feel the wall more dramatically, and have more runway for overtime rep salvage. The joke lands harder when you *almost* think you can do it.

**`TIMER_BY_DAY` proposed values in tuning.ts:**

```ts
TIMER_BY_DAY: {
  1: 40, 2: 40, 3: 38,           // tutorial — generous
  4: 42,                          // easy — first real prompts
  5: 50, 6: 50,                   // moderate — pressure building
  7: 60, 8: 55,                   // pressure — tight for 60 WPM
  9: 65, 10: 88,                  // hard — need good typing
  11: 110, 12: 120,               // brutal — every second counts
  13: 60,                         // impossible (intentional) — longer timer makes the wall feel dramatic, not just sudden
} as Record<number, number>,
```

**Reasoning:** Timers scale with actual char content so a 60 WPM typist hits the sweet spot: trivially easy early, increasing pressure mid-game, genuinely hard late. The current flat 45s creates a cliff at day 7 instead of a ramp.

### Corporate Dev Timer Implications

With `CORP_TIMER_RATIO: 0.49`, Corporate Dev gets:

| Day | Normal Timer | Corp Timer (49%) |
|-----|-------------|------------------|
| 1   | 40s         | 20s              |
| 7   | 60s         | 29s              |
| 10  | 88s         | 43s              |
| 12  | 120s        | 59s              |

This feels right — Corp Dev's challenge is the timer, not money. At 60 WPM, a Corp Dev can complete days 1–6 but struggles from day 7 onward (282 chars in 29s = 9.7 cps, requiring ~116 WPM). This is the "3★ Hard" experience.

## 3. Class Difficulty Star Ratings & Multipliers

### Current State

| Class | Mult | Budget | HP | Model | Slots | Quirks |
|-------|------|--------|----|-------|-------|--------|
| College Student | ×3.0 | $200 | 50 | free | 1 | — |
| Indie Hacker | ×1.8 | $800 | 80 | standard | 1 | — |
| Corporate Dev | ×1.2 | $99,999 | 90 | standard | 1 | Half timer, locked strategies, locked models |
| Tech Bro | ×0.8 | $2,000 | 100 | standard | 2 | Local model unlocked |

### Critical Design Issue: The Multiplier Is Decorative

The rank calculation at `ScoringSystem.calcFinalScore()` uses `rawTotal / maxPossibleRaw` (2730) — the class multiplier is NOT used for rank determination. It only affects the displayed `finalScore` number. This means:

- A College Student and Tech Bro who play identically get the **same rank**.
- The multiplier creates a cosmetic high-score number but has zero gameplay impact on the only thing players care about: their letter grade.

**This is actually fine** as long as difficulty comes from mechanical constraints (budget, timer, model locks), not the multiplier. The multiplier becomes a "mastery score" — Tech Bro's S-rank final score is 3.75× lower than College Student's, which matters for leaderboards/bragging but not for the rank narration.

### Proposed Star Ratings

| Star | Class | Difficulty Source |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| **1★ Easy** | **College Student** | High multiplier paper-scores high. Budget is tight ($200) but free model has only a -15% quality penalty. Days 1–6 require zero economic thought. Days 7+ require bug bounty farming to upgrade models, but the penalty for staying on free tier is small. |
| **2★ Medium** | **Indie Hacker** | $800 budget runs out by day ~10 on standard+justStart ($60/day). Must choose: downgrade to cheaper model (rep penalty) or farm bug bounty. Model/strategy choices matter from day 5 onward. |
| **3★ Hard** | **Corporate Dev** | Half timer is punishing. From day 7 onward (282 chars in 29s), a 60 WPM typist can't complete all prompts. Locked out of vibeCode strategy and cheap models. Infinite budget is offset by the timer crunch — you have the *best* tools but no *time* to use them. |
| **4★ Expert** | **Tech Bro** | Lowest multiplier (cosmetic). 2 agent slots is nice but doesn't compensate for the core challenge: $2,000 budget sounds generous but frontier ($100/day) + planThenBuild ($60/day) bleeds $160/day = bankrupt by day 12. Must optimize *everything*: farm bug bounty, time model upgrades, use local model (unique unlock) strategically. |

### Proposed Multiplier Adjustments

**Current:** 3.0 / 1.8 / 1.2 / 0.8

**Proposed:** 4.0 / 2.0 / 1.0 / 0.6

| Class | Current | Proposed | Reasoning |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|
| College Student | ×3.0 | **×4.0** | Widen the gap — a perfect College Student run should produce a dramatically high number that feels like "wow, I squeezed everything out of nothing." |
| Indie Hacker | ×1.8 | **×2.0** | Clean number. The balanced class gets a balanced multiplier. |
| Corporate Dev | ×1.2 | **×1.0** | The half-timer is the challenge, not the score. ×1.0 means "what you earn is what you get" — fitting for a corporate environment. |
| Tech Bro | ×0.8 | **×0.6** | Intentional punishment. Tech Bro has the most resources but the lowest score ceiling. Mastering Tech Bro to S-rank is a flex — your final score will be ~6.7× lower than College Student's, but you did it with style. The 2-agent-slot advantage and local model access must earn their keep through mechanical play, not score inflation. |

**Reasoning for wider range (4.0–0.6 vs 3.0–0.8):** The multiplier is decorative for rank but meaningful for bragging. A wider spread makes class choice feel more dramatic and makes leaderboard positioning interesting.

### Proposed Starting Budget Adjustments

| Class | Current | Proposed | Reasoning |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|
| College Student | $200 | **$150** | Should feel broke from day 1. $150 means free model is the *only* option for the first 4–5 days unless bug bounty is farmed aggressively. |
| Indie Hacker | $800 | **$600** | Tighter. $600 / $60/day (standard + justStart) = 10 days before bankruptcy. Forces a model downgrade or bug bounty run by day 8–9. |
| Corporate Dev | $99,999 | **$99,999** | Unchanged. Infinite money, no time. |
| Tech Bro | $2,000 | **$2,000** | Unchanged. $2,000 is enough to run frontier for ~12 days with oneShot ($110/day), but doing so leaves zero margin for strategy costs or Token Market purchases. The tension is right. |

## 4. Rank Threshold Overhaul

### Current System

Rank is `rawTotal / 2730` (sum of all maxReputation). With accuracy/strategy/model bonuses, a perfect run yields ~4041 raw = 148%. The bonuses push well past 100%, making ranks very generous.

| Rank | Current Threshold | Raw Rep Needed | Notes |
|------|-------------------|----------------|-------|
| S    | 90%               | 2,457          | Achievable by completing ~60% of prompts with good bonuses |
| A    | 75%               | 2,048          | Achievable by completing ~50% of prompts |
| B    | 60%               | 1,638          | |
| C    | 45%               | 1,229          | |
| D    | 30%               | 819            | |
| F    | <30%              | <819           | |

### The Problem

Because bonuses (accuracy 30%, strategy 15%, model 5–15%) stack multiplicatively on base rep, the effective max raw score is ~148% of the nominal max. A player who types well through day 8 and barely survives days 9–13 can still hit S-rank. The ranks don't create enough separation.

### Proposed Thresholds

Recalibrate against realistic play:
- A perfect run (all prompts, 95% accuracy, planThenBuild, frontier model) yields ~4041 raw.
- A "good" run (all prompts through day 10, 80% on 11–12, Day 13 chaos, 90% accuracy, standard model, justStart) yields ~3200 raw.
- A "decent" run (complete through day 8, struggling on 9–12, Day 13 chaos) yields ~2400 raw.

| Rank | Current | Proposed | Raw Rep Needed (new) | Who Gets This |
|------|---------|----------|----------------------|---------------|
| S    | 90%     | **115%** | 3,140                | Near-perfect play across all 13 days. Must complete hard days AND optimize model/strategy. |
| A    | 75%     | **90%**  | 2,457                | Good typer + good decisions. Complete most prompts through day 12. |
| B    | 60%     | **70%**  | 1,911                | Decent typing, reasonable model/strategy choices. Falls apart on days 11–12. |
| C    | 45%     | **50%**  | 1,365                | Average typer, minimal optimization. Completes easy/moderate days. |
| D    | 30%     | **30%**  | 819                  | Struggled throughout but survived. |
| F    | <30%    | **<30%** | <819                 | Did not survive. |

**Implementation (ScoringSystem.ts, ~line 106):**

```ts
// Current:
if (percentage >= 0.90) rank = 'S';
else if (percentage >= 0.75) rank = 'A';
else if (percentage >= 0.60) rank = 'B';
else if (percentage >= 0.45) rank = 'C';
else if (percentage >= 0.30) rank = 'D';

// Proposed:
if (percentage >= 1.15) rank = 'S';
else if (percentage >= 0.90) rank = 'A';
else if (percentage >= 0.70) rank = 'B';
else if (percentage >= 0.50) rank = 'C';
else if (percentage >= 0.30) rank = 'D';
```

**Reasoning:** S-rank should feel earned. Requiring 115% means you need the bonus multipliers firing AND high completion. This makes model choice and strategy meaningfully gate-keep the top rank — you can't S-rank on typing alone, you need good decisions too.

## 5. Token Market Rebalance

### Current Assessment

The Token Market is well-designed structurally (good category spread, joke items for flavor, consumables for micro-decisions). But the economic pressure is too loose for most classes:

- **College Student ($150 proposed):** Can barely afford anything. Bug bounty is the *only* income. Good — this is the intended constraint.
- **Indie Hacker ($600 proposed):** Can afford standard model indefinitely ($30/day × 13 = $390). Token Market purchases feel optional.
- **Corporate Dev:** Immune to costs. Token Market is irrelevant except for agent slots.
- **Tech Bro ($2,000):** Can afford frontier for most of the game. Token Market hardware upgrades feel like nice-to-haves, not necessities.

### Proposed Model Price Adjustments (Token Market unlock costs)

| Item | Current | Proposed | Reasoning |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| model-standard | $100 | **$80** | Slightly cheaper — College Student should be able to afford this after 2–3 good bug bounty sessions. First meaningful upgrade goal. |
| model-frontier | $300 | **$400** | More expensive — frontier's +15% quality bonus is the biggest single rep modifier in the game. Should feel like a major investment. |
| model-local | $200 | **$150** | Cheaper — local is Tech Bro's unique advantage. Other classes buying it should feel like they're getting a budget option ($0/day cost), not a premium item. |
| model-sketchy | $50 | **$30** | Impulse buy — sketchy is a gamble. Low unlock cost makes it tempting. The real cost is the -10% quality hit. |
| model-opensource | $150 | **$120** | Slightly cheaper — open source is the "responsible choice" ($10/day, 0% quality mod). Good midrange option. |

### Proposed Model Daily Cost Adjustments

| Model | Current $/day | Proposed $/day | Quality Mod | Reasoning |
|-------|---------------|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| free | $0 | **$0** | -15% | Unchanged — the baseline. |
| sketchy | $5 | **$5** | -10% | Unchanged — cheap but risky. |
| local | $0 | **$0** | -5% | Unchanged — hardware cost is the gate. |
| openSource | $10 | **$10** | 0% | Unchanged. |
| standard | $30 | **$40** | +5% | **Increase.** At $30/day, Indie Hacker can run standard for 20 days. At $40/day, it's 15 days — still comfortable but forces one downgrade decision near the end. |
| frontier | $100 | **$120** | +15% | **Increase.** Frontier is the best quality mod. At $120/day, Tech Bro ($2,000) can only run it for ~12 days after buying the unlock ($400). Forces strategic timing of when to use frontier vs standard. |

### Proposed Hardware Price Adjustments

| Item | Current | Proposed | Reasoning |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| hw-keyboard | $50 | **$35** | The mechanical keyboard's typo forgiveness is most valuable on hard days. Should be accessible early. First "must-buy" for any class. |
| hw-monitor | $75 | **$75** | Unchanged — +5% speed is solid value. |
| hw-gpu | $300 | **$250** | Slightly cheaper — GPU upgrade only matters for local/openSource users. Tech Bro should be able to afford this by day 3–4 from bug bounty. |
| hw-ram | $100 | **$100** | Unchanged — situational defense item. |
| hw-ups | $150 | **$150** | Unchanged — insurance item. |
| hw-desk | $120 | **$120** | Unchanged — hardware damage reduction is niche but valuable. |
| hw-cooling | $60 | **$60** | Unchanged. |

### Proposed Agent Slot Prices

| Item | Current | Proposed | Reasoning |
|------|---------|----------|-----------|
| slot-2 | $200 | **$250** | Slightly more expensive — agent slots are a permanent power upgrade. For Indie Hacker/College Student, this should represent a real tradeoff against model upgrades. |
| slot-3 | $400 | **$500** | More expensive — 3 agents is a major power spike (synergy combos). Should require significant bug bounty farming for non-Corp classes. |

### Token Market Strategic Loop Assessment

**The loop: Bug Bounty → $ → Token Market → Better Model → More Rep**

This loop works mechanically but has a legibility problem: players don't intuitively connect "I bought frontier model" to "I earned 15% more rep." The model quality bonus is invisible during gameplay and only appears as a small line in Results.

**Recommendation (no code change, UI-only):** The Planning screen already shows model rep modifier. Make the Token Market item cards show explicit rep impact, e.g.:

> **Frontier Model** — $400
> *Maximum quality. Costs $120/day.*
> **+15% reputation bonus on all projects**

This makes the buy/earn loop feel tangible.

// Current:

## 7. Strategy Mods & Costs

### Current Values (ScoringSystem.ts)

| Strategy | Rep Mod | Time Bonus | Cost/Day |
|----------|---------|------------|----------|
| planThenBuild | +15% | +6s | $60 |
| justStart | 0% | 0s | $30 |
| oneShot | -10% | -6s | $10 |
| vibeCode | random(-20% to +40%) | +3s | $45 |

### Assessment

- **planThenBuild** is dominant strategy — +15% rep AND +6s timer. There's no reason to pick anything else unless you can't afford $60/day.
- **justStart** is the "default" option but costs $30 for literally no bonus. It's a trap.
- **oneShot** is punishing (-10% rep AND -6s timer) but only saves $50 vs planThenBuild. Not worth it.
- **vibeCode** is chaotic but the +3s timer with avg +10% rep makes it strictly better than justStart.

### Proposed Changes

Make each strategy have a genuine niche:

| Strategy | Current Rep | Proposed Rep | Current Time | Proposed Time | Current Cost | Proposed Cost | Reasoning |
|----------|-------------|-------------|--------------|---------------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| planThenBuild | +15% | **+15%** | +6s | **+6s** | $60 | **$80** | Best rep. Make it expensive. The premium strategy for classes that can afford it. |
| justStart | 0% | **+5%** | 0s | **0s** | $30 | **$20** | Small rep bonus, cheap. The "safe default" should actually be good, not a trap. |
| oneShot | -10% | **-5%** | -6s | **-6s** | $10 | **$0** | Free but risky. The budget-conscious strategy. College Student's best friend. |
| vibeCode | -20%~+40% | **-20%~+40%** | +3s | **+3s** | $45 | **$40** | Unchanged mechanically. Slightly cheaper to make the gamble more tempting. |

**Implementation:**

In `ScoringSystem.ts`, add `justStart` bonus:
```ts
// Current:
case 'justStart': strategyMod = 0; break;
// Proposed:
case 'justStart': strategyMod = 0.05; break;
```

In `EconomySystem.ts` `getStrategyModifier()` and `PlanningScene.ts` strategy definitions, update costs:
```ts

// justStart:     strategyCost: 30 → 20
// oneShot:       strategyCost: 10 → 0
// vibeCode:      strategyCost: 45 → 40
```

Update the PlanningScene `riskLabel` strings to match new costs.

**Reasoning:** Every strategy should have a valid use case. Making oneShot free gives budget-strapped classes a real option (accept the rep/time penalty to save money for model upgrades). Making justStart slightly positive (+5%) means the "boring safe choice" isn't strictly worse than vibeCode.

## 8. Overtime Rep System

### Current: 3 rep per overtime prompt

The overtime system is the only way to salvage Day 13 (and to squeeze extra rep from easy days). At 3 rep/prompt with overtime prompts averaging ~25 chars:

- **Easy days (40s timer, 39 chars of real prompts):** ~25s of overtime = ~5 overtime prompts = +15 rep on a 50-max day. That's a 30% bonus — very significant!
- **Day 13 (60s, 731 chars):** A 60 WPM typist completes ~5/10 prompts (300 chars) in 60s — progress ~21%, yielding ~105 base rep. The timer feels long enough to build hope, then snatches it away. Overtime during remaining time adds 6–8 prompts = +30–40 rep. Total Day 13 rep for a solid player: ~140–150 on a 500-max day. That's a D-grade day, which feels right — you survived the chaos and salvaged something.

### Proposed Change

| Setting | Current | Proposed | Reasoning |
|---------|---------|----------|-----------|
| OVERTIME_REP_PER_PROMPT | 3 | **5** | Make overtime feel more rewarding, especially on Day 13. At 5 rep/prompt, a player who grinds 4 overtime prompts on Day 13 earns +20 rep — still small vs 500 max but psychologically it's "I salvaged something from the chaos." On early days, 5 overtime prompts = +25 rep on a 50-max day = 50% bonus, which makes fast typing feel very rewarding. |

**Reasoning:** Overtime is the game's pressure-release valve. Faster typists should feel meaningfully rewarded for finishing early. Bumping to 5 rep/prompt creates a bigger spread between "just finished" and "finished with 20s to spare."

## 11. Miscellaneous Observations

### 11a. Event Reputation Impacts Are Well-Distributed

Across 55 events and 322 reputation effects: average impact is +8.7 rep, with a range from -50 to +50. The distribution is slightly positive-biased (net +2812 across all effects), which is fine — events are interruptions, not punishments. Players should feel like events are risky but not hopeless.

**No changes recommended.**

### 11b. Bug Bounty Earnings Feel Right

Bug rewards ($12–$120 per bug, with combo multipliers and the 1.5× Old School bonus) mean a good bug bounty session earns $60–$200. This is enough to:
- Buy a mechanical keyboard ($35 proposed) after one session
- Fund 1–2 days of standard model ($40/day proposed) per session
- Save up for frontier unlock ($400 proposed) over 3–4 strong sessions

**No changes recommended.**

### 11c. Typo Forgiveness Curve Is Good

Days 1–2 get 2 forgiveness, day 3–5 get 1, days 6+ get 0. The mechanical keyboard adds 1 regardless. This creates a clear "training wheels → real challenge" arc.

**No changes recommended.**

### 11d. Event Read Window Is Aggressive on Late Days

Event read windows drop to 6–7s on days 11–13. With events triggering after nearly every prompt (9 events on day 13), players are spending significant time reading event dialogs during already-impossible timers. Since the timer pauses during events, this is mechanically fine but creates a pace issue — rapid-fire events feel more like a dialog simulator than a typing game.

**Consider:** Reduce Day 12 events from 7 to 5, and Day 13 events from 9 to 7. The chaos narrative is preserved with fewer events.

| Day | Current Events | Proposed Events | Reasoning |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|-----------|
| 12  | 7 (afterPrompts: [2,3,5,6,7,8,9]) | **5 (afterPrompts: [2,4,6,8,9])** | Spread events out more. Player types 2–3 prompts between events instead of 1. |
| 13  | 9 (afterPrompts: [2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,10]) | **7 (afterPrompts: [2,3,5,6,8,9,10])** | Still maximum pressure but with brief typing windows between event clusters. |

### 11e. Day 13 Overtime Feels Intentionally Chaotic ✅

With the proposed 5 rep/prompt overtime, Day 13 becomes: rush through 2–3 prompts (11% progress = 55 base rep), then grind overtime prompts for +5 rep each. A good typist might squeeze out 6–8 overtime prompts = +30–40 rep. Total: ~100–120 rep on a 500-max day. That's a C-grade day, which feels right for "the wheels came off but I survived."

### 11f. vibeCode Strategy Has Invisible Variance

vibeCode's rep modifier ranges from -20% to +40% (mean ~+10%), but the player has no feedback on what they rolled until the Results screen. During execution, there's no "vibeCode is going well" or "vibeCode is going badly" signal.

**Recommendation (nice-to-have, skip if tight on time):** Add a terminal message at ~50% progress:
- If roll > +15%: "🧠 The vibes are immaculate. Something beautiful is happening."
- If roll < -5%: "🧠 The vibes are... concerning. This might not end well."

### 11g. Joke Items Could Grant Small Rep Bonuses

Currently joke items just take your money and display a funny message. This is fine for 90% of them, but consider making 1–2 joke items grant a small hidden rep bonus (+3–5) to reward curious players.

**Candidate:** `joke-prompts` (Premium Prompt Pack, $30) — currently says "No mechanical benefit." Could grant +2 rep for one day as a tiny easter egg.
