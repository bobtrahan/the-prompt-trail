## Tech Bro
- **Theme:** Floating Hex / Overclocked / █ Cursor
- **Run Experience:** 
    - Early game is a breeze with $2,000 and 100 HP.
    - Tension only rises if "Let it mine" or "Push through" (overheating) are chosen repeatedly.
    - Model switching to local is a viable strategy thanks to high starting budget for GPU.
- **Fail-Pressure Band:** Late tension (Day 9+).
- **Balance Notes:** Strongest starting position. Multiplier (0.8x) keeps final scores competitive but safe.

## Indie Hacker
- **Theme:** Star Scatter / Budget / _ Cursor
- **Run Experience:**
    - Balanced and reactive. $800 is enough to survive Day 1-3 easily.
    - 1.8x multiplier makes every reputation point feel valuable.
    - "Product Hunt Launch" and "Hacker News" events are high-stakes swings.
- **Fail-Pressure Band:** Mid-game tension (Day 5+).
- **Balance Notes:** Feels like the "Standard" experience.

## College Student
- **Theme:** Notebook Doodles / Campus / | Cursor
- **Run Experience:**
    - High pressure from Day 1. $200 budget leaves no room for errors.
    - 50 HP hardware makes overheating or power flickers lethal.
    - 3.0x multiplier is the only thing keeping hope alive.
- **Fail-Pressure Band:** Early forgiveness, immediate tension (Day 1+).
- **Balance Notes:** Brutal but rewarding. Must use "Financial Aid" wisely.

## Corporate Dev
- **Theme:** Spreadsheet & CONFIDENTIAL / VPN / ▌ Cursor
- **Run Experience:**
    - Infinite money ($99,999) removes budget stress, but 22s timer (half-time) creates extreme typing pressure.
    - Mandatory meetings and reorgs are significant time sinks.
    - Multiplier (1.2x) is decent for the difficulty of the 22s timer.
- **Fail-Pressure Band:** Constant tension due to short timer.
- **Balance Notes:** Unique "Resource Swap" (Money for Time) works well.

**Overall Summary:** All classes reach their intended pressure bands. College Student is the most volatile. Corporate Dev is a pure typing skill check.
